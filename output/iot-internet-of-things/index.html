IoT (Internet of Things)

A network of interconnected devices capable of collecting and exchanging data without human intervention.

Description

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to a network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet. In the context of Non-Human Identities, IoT encompasses devices that operate autonomously, leveraging machine-to-machine communication. These devices can include smart appliances, industrial machinery, connected vehicles, and environmental sensors, all of which can monitor, analyze, and respond to their surroundings. IoT devices are characterized by their ability to gather real-time data, enabling improved efficiencies, predictive maintenance, and enhanced decision-making processes. The proliferation of IoT technology has led to the creation of vast data ecosystems where non-human entities can interact, learn, and adapt without direct human oversight. This shift not only transforms traditional industries but also raises questions around data privacy, security, and the ethical implications of autonomous decision-making.

Examples

  • Smart thermostats that learn user preferences and adjust heating/cooling automatically.
  • Wearable health monitors that track vital signs and send alerts to healthcare providers.

Additional Information

  • IoT devices can significantly reduce operational costs and increase efficiency in industries.
  • The growth of IoT is contributing to the development of smart cities, enhancing urban living through improved infrastructure and services.
