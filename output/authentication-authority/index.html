Authentication authority

An entity responsible for verifying the identity of non-human entities in a system.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, such as IoT devices, software applications, and automated services, an authentication authority serves as a critical component in ensuring secure interactions and communications. This authority issues, verifies, and manages credentials, such as tokens or certificates, that establish the identity of these non-human entities within a network. By ensuring that only authenticated entities can access systems and data, the authentication authority helps to mitigate risks associated with unauthorized access and potential security breaches. It plays a vital role in the broader identity and access management framework, enabling device-to-device communication as well as integration with human users. As non-human identities proliferate in various domains, including smart cities and industrial automation, the role of authentication authorities becomes increasingly important to maintain a secure and reliable ecosystem. Effective management of these identities is crucial for the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of services that depend on them.

Examples

  • Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) for IoT devices
  • OAuth 2.0 servers for application identities

Additional Information

  • Authentication authorities can be centralized or decentralized.
  • They may operate under various standards and protocols, such as X.509 or JWT.
