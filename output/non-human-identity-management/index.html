Non-Human Identity Management

The process of managing identities and access for non-human entities such as devices, applications, and services.

Description

Non-Human Identity Management refers to the systematic approach of identifying, managing, and securing the identities of non-human entities within an organization's digital ecosystem. This includes any entity that is not a human user, such as Internet of Things (IoT) devices, applications, APIs, and automated processes. As organizations increasingly adopt automation and interconnected systems, the need for effective Non-Human Identity Management becomes crucial. It ensures that these identities are properly authenticated, authorized, and monitored to prevent unauthorized access and potential security breaches. Non-Human Identity Management encompasses various practices, including the issuance of digital certificates, the use of tokens for API access, and the establishment of role-based access controls. By implementing robust management strategies, organizations can maintain security compliance, streamline operations, and enhance overall risk management. This management is essential for ensuring that non-human entities can interact securely and efficiently with other systems and services, contributing to the overall integrity and functionality of digital infrastructures.

Examples

  • IoT devices like smart sensors requiring unique identifiers and access controls.
  • APIs needing secure tokens for data exchange between services.

Additional Information

  • Non-Human Identity Management is critical in cloud environments where services interact autonomously.
  • It often involves automation tools for provisioning and de-provisioning identities at scale.
