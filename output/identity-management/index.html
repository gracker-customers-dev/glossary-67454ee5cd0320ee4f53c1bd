Identity management

The process of managing non-human identities in digital and physical environments.

Description

Identity management in the context of non-human identities involves the frameworks, policies, and technologies used to manage the identities of entities that are not human, such as devices, applications, and services. As organizations increasingly adopt Internet of Things (IoT) devices, cloud services, and automated systems, the need for effective identity management for these non-human identities has become critical. This encompasses the creation, maintenance, and deletion of digital identities, ensuring that each non-human identity is authenticated, authorized, and monitored. Identity management systems must also manage the relationships between these identities, ensuring they can interact securely and efficiently. Security protocols such as OAuth, SAML, and mutual TLS are often employed to protect these identities. The rise of AI and machine learning systems further complicates identity management as these systems may operate autonomously and require their own identity management solutions. Overall, effective identity management for non-human identities is essential for maintaining security, compliance, and operational efficiency in modern digital ecosystems.

Examples

  • IoT devices like smart thermostats that require unique identities for secure access.
  • Cloud services that use service accounts to manage permissions for non-human applications.

Additional Information

  • Identity management frameworks must comply with regulations like GDPR for data protection.
  • The automation of identity management processes can significantly reduce administrative overhead.
