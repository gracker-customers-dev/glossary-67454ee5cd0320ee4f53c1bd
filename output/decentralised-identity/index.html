Decentralised Identity

A digital identity that is managed and controlled by the individual or entity, rather than a centralized authority.

Description

Decentralised Identity refers to a system where identities are created, owned, and managed by individuals or non-human entities without relying on a central authority, such as governments or corporations. In the context of non-human identities, this concept extends to entities like IoT devices, applications, and organizations that require their own digital identities for interactions. With decentralised identity, these entities can authenticate themselves and establish trust in a peer-to-peer manner. This is typically achieved using blockchain technology or distributed ledger systems, which provide a secure and immutable record of identity claims. Non-human identities can benefit from decentralisation as it allows them to operate autonomously, make transactions, and communicate securely while maintaining control over their identity data. This approach mitigates risks associated with data breaches and central points of failure, enabling a more resilient and privacy-focused framework for identity management in an increasingly interconnected digital landscape.

Examples

  • An IoT device that authenticates itself to a network without relying on a central server.
  • A software application that uses a blockchain-based identity to interact with other services securely.

Additional Information

  • Decentralised identity can enhance security and privacy for both human and non-human entities.
  • It supports the concept of self-sovereign identity, where individuals or entities have full control over their identity data.
