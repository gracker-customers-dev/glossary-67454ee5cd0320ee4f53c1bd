Identity Threat Detection & Response (ITDR)

ITDR refers to the strategies and technologies used to identify, monitor, and respond to threats targeting non-human identities, such as service accounts, bots, and applications.

Description

Identity Threat Detection & Response (ITDR) focuses on safeguarding non-human identities that are essential to modern IT operations. Non-human identities include service accounts, machine identities, applications, and automated scripts that interact with systems and data. These identities can be exploited by malicious actors to gain unauthorized access, exfiltrate sensitive data, or execute harmful actions without detection. ITDR solutions employ advanced analytics, machine learning, and behavior monitoring to detect anomalous activities associated with non-human identities. This proactive approach enables organizations to quickly identify potential threats, assess their impact, and initiate appropriate response measures. ITDR not only enhances security posture but also ensures compliance with regulatory requirements by maintaining oversight over all identities, human or non-human. By focusing on non-human identities, ITDR helps reduce the attack surface and protect critical assets from sophisticated cyber threats.

Examples

  • Detection of unusual login patterns from a service account that could indicate credential theft.
  • Monitoring bot activities for signs of abuse, such as excessive API calls or access attempts to unauthorized resources.

Additional Information

  • ITDR solutions often integrate with existing security tools for unified visibility.
  • Regular audits of non-human identities are essential to maintaining security and compliance.
