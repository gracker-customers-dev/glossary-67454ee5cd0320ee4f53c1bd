Smart-card platform

A smart-card platform is a secure, integrated system that uses smart cards to manage and authenticate non-human identities in various applications.

Description

A smart-card platform refers to an infrastructure that utilizes smart cards—plastic cards embedded with integrated circuits—to facilitate secure access and identity management for non-human entities, such as devices, machines, or automated systems. These platforms are designed to ensure the integrity and authenticity of non-human identities by leveraging the cryptographic capabilities of smart cards. In various industries, including finance, healthcare, and telecommunications, smart-card platforms are employed to enhance security protocols, enabling automated systems to authenticate themselves before accessing resources or performing transactions. For example, in an Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem, devices can use smart cards to verify their identity and establish secure communication with other devices or cloud services. The use of smart-card platforms helps mitigate risks associated with identity theft and unauthorized access, as they offer a high level of security through encryption and secure key storage. This technology is pivotal in automating operations while maintaining stringent security measures.

Examples

  • Smart cards used in access control for automated machinery in manufacturing.
  • Smart card-based authentication for IoT devices in a smart home system.

Additional Information

  • Smart-card platforms can support multiple applications and identities on a single card.
  • They often comply with international security standards such as ISO/IEC 7816.
