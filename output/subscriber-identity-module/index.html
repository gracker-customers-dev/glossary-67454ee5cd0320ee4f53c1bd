Subscriber Identity Module

A Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) is a secure microcontroller embedded in mobile devices that stores subscriber information and enables connectivity to mobile networks.

Description

In the context of Non-Human Identities, the Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) serves as a critical component in the connectivity of Internet of Things (IoT) devices, machines, and systems that require mobile network access. Each SIM card contains unique identifiers such as the International Mobile Subscriber Identity (IMSI), which allows mobile networks to authenticate and authorize devices for communication. This is essential for non-human entities, such as smart sensors, autonomous vehicles, or connected appliances, as they rely on cellular networks for data transmission and remote control. The SIM enables these devices to establish secure connections, manage identity, and communicate efficiently over the network. Furthermore, the use of eSIM technology (embedded SIM) is becoming increasingly popular, allowing for more flexible and scalable deployment of non-human identities in various applications without the need for physical SIM card swaps. Overall, the SIM plays a vital role in ensuring that non-human devices can operate seamlessly within the mobile ecosystem.

Examples

  • A smart meter using a SIM to transmit energy consumption data to utility companies.
  • An autonomous drone utilizing a SIM card for real-time navigation and communication.

Additional Information

  • eSIM technology offers remote provisioning, eliminating the need for physical SIM cards.
  • SIM cards can also store data and applications, enhancing functionality in connected devices.
