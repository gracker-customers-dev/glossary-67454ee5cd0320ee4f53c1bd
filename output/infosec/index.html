InfoSec

Information Security (InfoSec) is the practice of protecting information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.

Description

In the context of Non-Human Identities, InfoSec refers to the strategies and technologies employed to safeguard data that is associated with digital entities that do not possess human characteristics, such as artificial intelligence systems, bots, and automated processes. These non-human identities often interact with and manage sensitive information, making it crucial to ensure their security protocols are robust and effective. InfoSec for non-human identities involves implementing measures like encryption, access controls, and continuous monitoring to protect against cyber threats. It also encompasses the management of data privacy and compliance with regulations that govern how information is handled, especially in scenarios where autonomous systems operate. As these non-human identities become more prevalent in various sectors, including finance, healthcare, and manufacturing, the importance of InfoSec increases to prevent data breaches and maintain trust in automated systems.

Examples

  • Automated trading systems that require secure access to financial data.
  • Chatbots managing customer information and requiring protection against data leaks.

Additional Information

  • The rise of IoT devices introduces new challenges for InfoSec as these devices often have non-human identities.
  • InfoSec frameworks must evolve to address the unique vulnerabilities associated with machine learning models and AI applications.
