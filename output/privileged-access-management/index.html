Privileged Access Management

A security discipline that manages and controls access rights for non-human identities.

Description

Privileged Access Management (PAM) refers to the strategies and tools used to secure, manage, and monitor access to critical systems and sensitive data, specifically focusing on non-human identities such as service accounts, application identities, and automation tools. These non-human identities often have elevated permissions that can pose significant security risks if compromised. PAM solutions help organizations to enforce the principle of least privilege, ensuring that non-human accounts only have the access necessary to perform their functions, and that their activities are logged and monitored. PAM also involves the use of password vaulting, session recording, and real-time threat detection to prevent unauthorized access and identify suspicious behavior. By managing these identities effectively, organizations can reduce the attack surface and comply with regulatory requirements, thereby enhancing their overall security posture.

Examples

  • Service accounts used by applications to access databases securely.
  • Automation scripts that require elevated permissions to perform system tasks.

Additional Information

  • PAM is critical for compliance with regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.
  • Effective PAM can help mitigate insider threats and external attacks.
