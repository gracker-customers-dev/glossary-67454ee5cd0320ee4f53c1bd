Vault

A secure repository for storing and managing non-human identities.

Description

In the context of Non-Human Identities, a 'Vault' refers to a secure environment where digital identities for non-human entities, such as applications, devices, and services, are stored and managed. These identities can include credentials, API keys, and other sensitive information that allows non-human entities to authenticate and interact with various systems securely. Vaults are essential in ensuring that non-human identities are protected from unauthorized access and are used in compliance with security policies. They often feature robust access controls, encryption techniques, and audit trails to monitor usage. The concept of a vault extends to various implementations, including cloud-based vault services and on-premise solutions, providing flexibility based on organizational needs. As non-human interactions become increasingly common in API-driven architectures and IoT ecosystems, the importance of secure vaults for managing these identities grows significantly in safeguarding digital operations.

Examples

  • HashiCorp Vault: A tool for securely accessing secrets and managing sensitive data.
  • AWS Secrets Manager: A service that helps you protect access to your applications, services, and IT resources without the upfront investment and on-going maintenance costs of operating your own infrastructure.

Additional Information

  • Vaults can implement rotation policies for credentials to enhance security.
  • Many vault solutions support integration with existing identity and access management systems.
