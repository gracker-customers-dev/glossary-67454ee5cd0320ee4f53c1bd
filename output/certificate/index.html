Certificate

A digital credential that verifies the identity and attributes of a non-human entity.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, a certificate serves as a digital credential that authenticates the identity of non-human entities like applications, devices, or services. These certificates are typically issued by trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs) and utilize cryptographic techniques to ensure secure communication and data integrity. Non-human identities often require a means of establishing trust when interacting with other systems or services, and certificates provide a standardized way to achieve this. For instance, a device connected to the Internet of Things (IoT) may possess a certificate that verifies its identity to other devices or cloud services, ensuring that communications are secure and that the device is authorized to participate in the network. Certificates can also encapsulate important attributes about the non-human identity, such as its permissions or capabilities, and are essential in scenarios like secure API communication, where trust needs to be established without direct human oversight.

Examples

  • An SSL/TLS certificate for a web server ensuring secure connections.
  • A digital certificate for an IoT device enabling secure communication with cloud services.

Additional Information

  • Certificates can be managed using Public Key Infrastructure (PKI).
  • Revocation mechanisms exist to invalidate compromised certificates.
