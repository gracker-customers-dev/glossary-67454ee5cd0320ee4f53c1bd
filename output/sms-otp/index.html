SMS-OTP

SMS-OTP stands for Short Message Service One-Time Password, a security mechanism used to verify identities.

Description

SMS-OTP is a widely used authentication method that involves the generation of a one-time password (OTP) sent via SMS to a registered mobile device. This process is critical for enhancing security, particularly for non-human identities such as automated systems, bots, or applications that require a secure way to verify their identity before accessing sensitive data or performing transactions. The OTP is usually a numeric code that expires after a short period or after a single use, minimizing the risk of unauthorized access. For non-human identities, SMS-OTP can be integrated into operations where automated systems need to authenticate their actions, ensuring that only authorized requests proceed. However, reliance on SMS can present vulnerabilities, such as interception or SIM swapping, making it essential to consider additional layers of security. Despite these concerns, SMS-OTP remains a popular choice due to its simplicity and widespread adoption across various platforms.

Examples

  • A bot that retrieves data from a secure API may require SMS-OTP verification to authenticate its identity.
  • An automated payment system could use SMS-OTP to confirm transactions initiated by non-human entities.

Additional Information

  • SMS-OTP is often used in two-factor authentication (2FA) processes.
  • Some organizations are moving towards app-based OTP systems for enhanced security over SMS.
