IAM (Identity and Access Management)

IAM refers to the policies and technologies that manage digital identities and control access to resources, particularly in relation to non-human identities such as applications, services, and devices.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, IAM (Identity and Access Management) encompasses the systems and processes that govern how non-human entities, such as applications, services, and devices, authenticate and authorize themselves to access resources within an IT environment. This is crucial as organizations increasingly rely on automation and integration between multiple systems. Non-human identities can include APIs, microservices, IoT devices, and other automated systems that require secure access to data and services without direct human intervention. Effective IAM solutions help ensure that these identities are granted the appropriate level of access based on their roles and requirements, thus minimizing security risks and ensuring compliance with regulations. By managing these non-human identities, organizations can better protect their sensitive data, maintain operational efficiency, and prevent unauthorized access while supporting the modern digital landscape.

Examples

  • API keys used for automated access to web services.
  • Machine identities in IoT devices that communicate with cloud services.

Additional Information

  • IAM for non-human identities often includes features like tokenization and certificate management.
  • Governance frameworks are essential to ensure the security and compliance of automated systems.
