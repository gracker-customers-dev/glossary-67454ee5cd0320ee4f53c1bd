Manual intervention

The process of human involvement in automated systems to manage, correct, or enhance operations.

Description

In the context of Non-Human Identities (NHIs), manual intervention refers to the need for human oversight or action in automated processes that involve digital entities or services. NHIs operate through algorithms and predefined rules, often lacking the ability to make nuanced decisions in complex or unforeseen situations. Therefore, when an NHI encounters a scenario that it cannot handle appropriately—such as data discrepancies, errors in processing, or unexpected user interactions—manual intervention becomes necessary. This human involvement can take many forms, from simple troubleshooting and error correction to more complex decision-making tasks. The goal of manual intervention is to ensure that the automated systems function correctly, maintain integrity, and provide value without compromising security or user experience. As organizations increasingly rely on NHIs for various operations, the importance of having an effective manual intervention strategy becomes crucial to mitigate risks and enhance overall efficiency.

Examples

  • A chatbot requires human support to resolve a customer issue it cannot understand.
  • An automated payment system flags a transaction for review, requiring a human to approve or deny it.

Additional Information

  • Manual intervention is critical in ensuring compliance with regulations and standards.
  • Effective manual intervention strategies can improve the performance and reliability of automated systems.
