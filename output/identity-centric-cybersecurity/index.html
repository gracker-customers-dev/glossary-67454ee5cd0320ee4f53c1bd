Identity-centric cybersecurity

A cybersecurity approach focused on managing and protecting the identities of users and non-human entities within a digital environment.

Description

Identity-centric cybersecurity emphasizes the importance of identities, both human and non-human, in securing systems and data. Non-human identities refer to digital entities such as applications, devices, APIs, and bots that require access to resources and services. This approach recognizes that traditional perimeter-based security is no longer sufficient, as threats can originate from inside or outside the network. By implementing identity-centric strategies, organizations can enforce strict access controls based on the identity and context of the user or entity requesting access. This includes verifying the identity through multi-factor authentication, implementing role-based access controls, and continuously monitoring for unusual behavior. By focusing on identity, organizations can better manage risks associated with credential theft, unauthorized access, and insider threats. The increasing use of cloud services and IoT devices has made non-human identities more prevalent, necessitating robust identity management solutions to ensure that all entities are authenticated and authorized properly.

Examples

  • An API that requires authentication tokens to access sensitive data.
  • A smart device that uses unique identifiers to connect securely to a network.

Additional Information

  • Identity-centric approaches often utilize technologies such as identity and access management (IAM) and zero trust security.
  • Effective management of non-human identities is crucial for securing modern digital infrastructures, including cloud environments.
