IAM

Identity and Access Management (IAM) related to non-human identities.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, Identity and Access Management (IAM) refers to the frameworks and technologies used to manage digital identities that are not associated with human users. This includes automated systems, applications, and devices that require secure, controlled access to resources in an organization's IT environment. Non-human identities can include service accounts, application identities, robots, and IoT devices. IAM solutions for non-human identities ensure that these entities are authenticated, authorized, and audited, similar to human users, but with additional considerations for automation and scale. Effective IAM for non-human identities is critical for maintaining security, ensuring compliance with regulations, and managing risk. It involves the use of policies, technologies, and processes to control access to systems and data while also enabling the efficient operation of automated processes and services.

Examples

  • Service accounts used for automated software deployments.
  • IoT devices that connect to cloud services.

Additional Information

  • IAM solutions can help prevent unauthorized access and data breaches.
  • Proper management of non-human identities is essential for cloud security.
