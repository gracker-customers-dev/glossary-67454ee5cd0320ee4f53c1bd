CIA Triad

A model for understanding the core principles of information security.

Description

The CIA Triad refers to three fundamental principles of information security: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. In the context of non-human identities, such as AI systems, IoT devices, and automated bots, these principles take on unique implications. Confidentiality ensures that sensitive data processed or generated by non-human entities is protected from unauthorized access, which is crucial for maintaining privacy and compliance with regulations. Integrity involves ensuring that the data these entities handle remains accurate and unaltered, as compromised data can lead to erroneous decisions or actions. Availability ensures that these systems and their data are accessible when needed, which is critical for operational continuity. The CIA Triad serves as a foundational framework to assess and mitigate risks associated with non-human identities, guiding the development of security protocols that protect against threats such as data breaches, system failures, and unauthorized manipulations. As the reliance on non-human identities grows, understanding and implementing the CIA Triad becomes increasingly important for organizations aiming to safeguard their digital environments.

Examples

  • AI chatbots handling customer data must ensure confidentiality to protect user privacy.
  • IoT devices in smart homes must maintain integrity to ensure accurate sensor readings.

Additional Information

  • The CIA Triad is foundational for developing security policies for automated systems.
  • Threats to non-human identities can include hacking, malware, and data poisoning.
