Service account

A service account is a non-human identity used to perform automated tasks or access resources in software applications and services.

Description

Service accounts are specialized accounts that are used to authenticate and authorize automated processes rather than human users. They are commonly utilized in cloud environments, application development, and system integration. Service accounts allow applications to securely interact with APIs, access databases, and perform background jobs without requiring human intervention. Unlike regular user accounts, service accounts typically have specific permissions tailored to their role, minimizing the risk associated with over-privileged access. These accounts can be configured with varying levels of access rights, which can help enforce security policies and ensure that only necessary permissions are granted. Additionally, service accounts can be managed and monitored, allowing organizations to track their usage and ensure compliance with security standards. This mechanism is essential in environments that rely on automation and microservices architecture, where services need to communicate securely without the overhead of human authentication processes.

Examples

  • A cloud service account that allows a web application to access storage resources.
  • A CI/CD pipeline service account that interacts with version control systems and deployment platforms.

Additional Information

  • Service accounts often use key-based authentication rather than password-based authentication.
  • Regularly rotating keys and auditing permissions are best practices for managing service accounts.
