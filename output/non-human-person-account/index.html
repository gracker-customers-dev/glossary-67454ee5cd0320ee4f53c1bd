Non-human/person account

An account or identity that represents a non-human entity, such as an organization, animal, or fictional character, rather than a human individual.

Description

In the realm of digital identity, a non-human/person account refers to profiles or accounts that do not correspond to real human beings but instead represent entities such as businesses, pets, fictional characters, or even automated bots. These accounts can serve various purposes, including marketing, community engagement, or providing information and entertainment. For example, a company may have a social media account to interact with customers, while a pet might have an account to share their daily adventures. Non-human/person accounts can be beneficial for organizations to establish a brand presence or for community initiatives. However, they can also raise issues concerning authenticity, as the distinction between human and non-human accounts might not always be clear. This raises questions about representation, transparency, and the nature of online interactions. Understanding the implications of non-human identities is crucial in today's digital landscape, where the lines between human and non-human accounts increasingly blur.

Examples

  • A Twitter account for a fictional character promoting a book.
  • An Instagram account for a pet sharing daily photos and videos.

Additional Information

  • Non-human accounts can engage with users in similar ways to human accounts.
  • They may be subject to different rules and regulations on various platforms.
