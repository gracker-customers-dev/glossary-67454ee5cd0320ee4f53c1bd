Authentication Level

The degree of assurance that a non-human identity is valid and authorized to perform specific actions.

Description

In the context of non-human identities, such as service accounts, IoT devices, or API clients, the 'Authentication Level' refers to the established trustworthiness of these identities when accessing systems or performing actions. Non-human identities often require different authentication mechanisms compared to human users due to their unique operational requirements and security needs. The authentication level can vary based on the sensitivity of the resources being accessed, the context of the operation, and the potential risks involved. For example, a low-level authentication may suffice for a public API, while a high-level authentication involving multifactor mechanisms may be necessary for accessing sensitive data or performing critical operations. Establishing appropriate authentication levels helps organizations mitigate risks associated with unauthorized access and ensures that non-human entities are properly verified before they can execute actions or access protected resources.

Examples

  • A service account using OAuth 2.0 tokens to authenticate with an API.
  • An IoT device utilizing certificate-based authentication to connect to a cloud service.

Additional Information

  • Authentication levels can be defined by attributes such as token type, expiration, and scope.
  • Different industries may have regulatory requirements that dictate specific authentication levels for non-human identities.
