Contextual Visibility

The degree to which non-human identities are recognized and understood within specific contexts.

Description

Contextual Visibility refers to how non-human entities, such as artificial intelligence, animals, or plants, are perceived and acknowledged in various environments and situations. This concept emphasizes that the visibility of these identities is not static; instead, it fluctuates based on the context in which they are situated. For instance, an AI may be highly visible in a tech conference setting, where its capabilities are discussed and showcased, but may be less visible in a social context where human interactions are prioritized. Similarly, a domesticated animal may have high visibility in a household setting, serving as a companion, while its role may be less recognized in agricultural settings. The implications of Contextual Visibility are significant for understanding how society interacts with and attributes value to non-human identities, impacting ethical considerations, policy-making, and the development of technology. Recognizing these shifts in visibility can lead to more inclusive approaches that account for the roles and rights of non-human entities across various domains.

Examples

  • An AI assistant being recognized as a valuable tool in a business meeting.
  • A therapy dog being acknowledged for its role in mental health support during a therapy session.

Additional Information

  • Contextual Visibility can influence public perception and acceptance of non-human entities.
  • Increasing awareness of Contextual Visibility can lead to improved ethical standards regarding non-human rights.
