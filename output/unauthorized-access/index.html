Unauthorized Access

The act of gaining entry to a system or network without permission, particularly in relation to non-human identities such as bots, scripts, or automated agents.

Description

Unauthorized access occurs when a non-human entity, such as a bot, script, or automated agent, attempts to enter a system, network, or database without legitimate credentials or authorization. This can happen through various means, including exploiting vulnerabilities, using stolen credentials, or bypassing security measures. Non-human identities can automate attacks that target weaknesses in systems, allowing them to traverse security barriers more efficiently than human attackers. This poses significant risks to information security, as unauthorized access can lead to data breaches, theft of sensitive information, or disruption of services. Organizations must implement robust security measures such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and access controls to mitigate these risks. Additionally, monitoring and analyzing traffic for unusual patterns can help detect and prevent unauthorized access by non-human entities. As technology evolves, so do the methods of unauthorized access, necessitating continuous adaptation of security strategies to protect sensitive data and resources.

Examples

  • A bot attempting to scrape data from a web application without permission.
  • An automated script exploiting a software vulnerability to gain access to a database.

Additional Information

  • Unauthorized access can lead to significant financial and reputational damage for organizations.
  • Non-human identities are increasingly used in cyberattacks due to their ability to operate at scale and speed.
