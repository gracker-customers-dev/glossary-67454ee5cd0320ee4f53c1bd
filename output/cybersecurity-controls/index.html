Cybersecurity controls

Measures implemented to protect systems and data associated with non-human identities.

Description

Cybersecurity controls refer to the various safeguards and measures put in place to protect information systems and data from unauthorized access, breaches, and other cyber threats, specifically in the context of non-human identities such as bots, APIs, and automated systems. Non-human identities are increasingly utilized in digital environments, performing tasks that were traditionally executed by human users. Therefore, it's essential to establish robust cybersecurity controls tailored to these identities to ensure they operate securely and do not become vectors for attacks. These controls may include authentication mechanisms, role-based access control, encryption, and monitoring systems designed to detect anomalous behavior in automated interactions. By implementing effective cybersecurity controls, organizations can mitigate risks associated with the misuse of non-human identities and enhance overall security posture, ensuring that automated processes do not compromise sensitive data or system integrity.

Examples

  • API rate limiting to prevent abuse of non-human interactions.
  • Implementation of OAuth for secure API access.

Additional Information

  • Regular audits of non-human identity activities to detect anomalies.
  • Use of machine learning to identify unusual patterns in automated transactions.
