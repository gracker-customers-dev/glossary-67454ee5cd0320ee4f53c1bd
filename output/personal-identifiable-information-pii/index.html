Personal Identifiable Information (PII)

Data that can be used to identify an individual, whether human or non-human.

Description

Personal Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that can be used to identify a specific individual. In the context of non-human identities, PII extends to information that distinguishes non-human entities such as organizations, brands, or even automated systems. For instance, a company's registration number, an entity's unique digital signature, or a machine's IP address can serve as PII because they uniquely identify that entity in a digital landscape. This concept has gained importance with the rise of digital identities, where non-human entities operate in online environments. Protecting this information is crucial to maintain privacy and security, as misuse can lead to unauthorized access, identity theft, or reputational damage. Organizations must implement robust data handling practices to safeguard PII, especially as regulatory frameworks evolve to address the complexities of non-human digital identities, ensuring compliance and ethical use of information.

Examples

  • Company registration number
  • Unique digital identifiers for automated systems

Additional Information

  • Non-human PII is increasingly relevant in digital commerce and AI.
  • Regulations like GDPR also apply to non-human identities in certain contexts.
