Circle of Trust

A conceptual framework for understanding the relationships and boundaries individuals establish with non-human entities.

Description

The 'Circle of Trust' refers to the informal boundaries that humans create to determine the extent of their emotional, social, and ethical interactions with non-human identities, such as artificial intelligences, robots, and other non-human agents. This concept recognizes that as technology evolves, individuals increasingly engage with non-human entities that exhibit social behaviors or emotional responses. The Circle of Trust is established when individuals feel comfortable interacting with these entities, often based on their perceived reliability, intelligence, and ability to understand human emotions. This trust can be influenced by various factors, including the entity's design, functionality, and the context of the interaction. As individuals include non-human identities within their Circle of Trust, it raises important questions about ethical considerations, accountability, and the implications of such relationships on human behavior and society.

Examples

  • A person forming a bond with a virtual assistant like Siri or Alexa, relying on it for daily tasks.
  • A child developing an emotional connection with a robotic pet, treating it as a companion.

Additional Information

  • The concept highlights the need for ethical guidelines in AI development.
  • It encourages discussions on the implications of trust in technology on human relationships.
