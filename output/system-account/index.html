System account

A non-human identity associated with automated processes and services within a computing environment.

Description

System accounts are specialized accounts used by software applications, services, and automated processes to perform tasks without human intervention. These accounts typically have predefined permissions and access rights tailored to the specific needs of the application or service they support. Unlike human user accounts, system accounts do not have personal identifiers and are not associated with a specific individual. They are essential for enabling secure and efficient operation of various systems, such as databases, web servers, and cloud services. System accounts can be categorized based on their functionality, such as service accounts, application accounts, or daemon accounts. Proper management of system accounts is critical to maintaining security, as they can be targeted by attackers seeking to exploit vulnerabilities in automated processes. Regular audits, strict access controls, and adherence to the principle of least privilege are vital practices for safeguarding system accounts.

Examples

  • A Windows service account used to run background services.
  • An AWS IAM role assigned to a Lambda function for accessing other AWS resources.

Additional Information

  • System accounts should have passwords that are regularly updated and managed securely.
  • Monitoring and logging activities associated with system accounts can help detect unauthorized access.
