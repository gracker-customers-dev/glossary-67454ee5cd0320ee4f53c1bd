OT (Operational Technology)

Operational Technology (OT) refers to hardware and software that detects or causes changes through direct monitoring and control of physical devices, processes, and events.

Description

Operational Technology (OT) encompasses a variety of systems and technologies used to monitor and control physical processes, devices, and infrastructure in industries such as manufacturing, energy, transportation, and utilities. Unlike Information Technology (IT), which focuses on data and information systems, OT is primarily concerned with the physical processes and the equipment that operate them. OT includes technologies such as Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems, Distributed Control Systems (DCS), and Industrial Control Systems (ICS). These systems are crucial for the operation of critical infrastructure, enabling the automation of processes, real-time monitoring, and remote control of machinery and systems. In the context of Non-Human Identities, OT represents the digital identities and systems that manage these physical processes, interacting with physical machinery and environments. As industries increasingly integrate IoT (Internet of Things) devices, the convergence of OT and IT is leading to new challenges in cybersecurity and data management, necessitating robust frameworks for risk assessment and operational integrity.

Examples

  • SCADA systems in water treatment plants
  • Automated assembly lines in manufacturing facilities

Additional Information

  • OT systems often operate in real-time and require high availability.
  • Cybersecurity threats to OT can have serious implications for safety and operational continuity.
