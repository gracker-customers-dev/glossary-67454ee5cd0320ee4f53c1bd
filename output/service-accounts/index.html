Service Accounts

Service accounts are non-human identities used by applications or services to authenticate and interact with systems and services.

Description

Service accounts are specialized accounts designed for automated processes and applications rather than individual users. These accounts facilitate secure, non-interactive authentication to perform tasks, access resources, and manage services without user intervention. They are essential in cloud computing, DevOps, and microservices architectures, allowing applications to communicate with each other securely. Unlike regular user accounts, service accounts typically have restricted permissions tailored to specific operational needs, minimizing security risks. They can be used for various purposes, including running background jobs, accessing APIs, or managing resources in cloud environments. Properly managing service accounts, including rotating credentials and applying the principle of least privilege, is crucial to maintaining security. Unlike traditional user accounts, they do not require a password reset, but their keys or tokens must be managed securely to prevent unauthorized access.

Examples

  • A cloud application using a service account to access a database.
  • An automated CI/CD pipeline utilizing service accounts to deploy applications.

Additional Information

  • Service accounts can be created with specific roles and permissions to limit their access.
  • They often use OAuth tokens, API keys, or certificates for authentication.
