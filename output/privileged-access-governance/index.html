Privileged access governance

A framework for managing and securing access rights of non-human identities to critical systems and data.

Description

Privileged access governance refers to the policies, processes, and technologies that organizations employ to manage and monitor access rights, particularly for non-human identities such as service accounts, applications, and automation tools. These non-human identities often require elevated privileges to perform automated tasks or access sensitive data, making them attractive targets for cyber threats. Effective governance ensures that these identities only have the minimum necessary access, which is regularly reviewed and audited. It also involves implementing controls such as session monitoring, access request workflows, and the use of privileged access management (PAM) solutions to track and manage these accounts. In the context of cybersecurity, strong privileged access governance mitigates the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access resulting from compromised non-human identities. This governance is essential in fostering compliance with regulatory standards and protecting organizational assets.

Examples

  • Restricting access rights for a cloud service account to only the necessary resources.
  • Implementing automated workflows for requesting and approving access to sensitive data by applications.

Additional Information

  • Non-human identities often operate without direct human oversight, increasing the risk of misuse.
  • Regular audits and monitoring are critical to maintaining the integrity of privileged access governance.
